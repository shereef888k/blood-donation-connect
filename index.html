âœ… Eligibility Criteria

  • Age: 18-65 years
  • Weight: Minimum 50 kg
  • Good general health
  • No recent illness or medication
  • Gap of 3 months between donations

ğŸ©¸ Blood Group Compatibility

Universal Donor: O- (can donate to all)

Universal Recipient: AB+ (can receive from all)

A+: Can donate to A+, AB+

B+: Can donate to B+, AB+

O+: Can donate to A+, B+, AB+, O+

ğŸ’¡ Preparation Tips

  • Get adequate sleep (8+ hours)
  • Eat iron-rich foods
  • Stay well hydrated
  • Avoid alcohol 24 hours before
  • Wear comfortable clothing

ğŸ“ Donation Process

  1. Registration and basic health screening
  2. Hemoglobin and blood pressure check
  3. Blood donation (usually 350-450 ml)
  4. Rest and refreshments for 10-15 minutes
  5. Receive a donor certificate or card

ğŸ›¡ï¸ Post-Donation Care

  • Rest for a few minutes after donating
  • Drink extra fluids for the next 24 hours
  • Avoid heavy exercise for the rest of the day
  • If you feel dizzy, lie down until you feel better

â“ Myths & Facts

  • Myth: Donating blood makes you weak.
    Fact: Your body replaces the lost blood within a few days.
  • Myth: You can get infections from donating.
    Fact: Sterile, single-use equipment is always used.
  • Myth: Only rare blood groups are needed.
    Fact: All blood groups are important and needed.

ğŸ™‹ Frequently Asked Questions

  • How often can I donate? Every 3 months for men, every 4 months for women.
  • Is blood donation painful? Only a slight pinch is felt during needle insertion.
  • Can I donate if I have a tattoo? Yes, if it was done more than 6 months ago.
